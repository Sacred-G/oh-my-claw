# Title 17 Compliance Reference

## Key Distinction
- **Title 17**: Community Care Licensing - applies to supported living agencies providing services to consumers in their own homes
- **Title 22**: NOT applicable to supported living services (applies to skilled nursing facilities and group homes)

## Core Requirements

### Consumer Rights
Consumers receiving supported living services have specific rights including:
- Right to dignity and respect
- Right to make informed choices about services
- Right to privacy and confidentiality
- Right to be free from abuse and neglect
- Right to file grievances without retaliation

### Staffing Requirements

#### Direct Support Staff (DSS)
- Use term "Direct Support Staff" NOT "Direct Support Professional"
- Must complete orientation within 7 days of hire
- Must receive ongoing training and supervision
- Background clearance required (Live Scan, criminal record clearance)
- Tuberculosis testing/clearance required

#### Staff Training Requirements
- Orientation training (minimum requirements)
- Person-centered planning
- Consumer rights and advocacy
- Confidentiality and HIPAA
- Emergency procedures and safety
- Mandated reporter training (abuse/neglect reporting)
- Medication observation training (if applicable)

### Eight Rights of Medication Observation
Direct Support Staff who observe medication administration must verify:
1. Right Consumer
2. Right Medication
3. Right Dose
4. Right Time
5. Right Route
6. Right Documentation
7. Right to Refuse
8. Right Reason

Note: Staff observe but do NOT administer medications in supported living settings.

### Documentation Requirements
- Individual Service Plans (ISPs)
- Progress notes and service documentation
- Incident reports (within 24 hours for serious incidents)
- Medication observation logs
- Training records for all staff
- Consumer rights acknowledgments
- Emergency contact information

### Regional Center Vendor Requirements
- Active vendor status with Regional Center
- Compliance with Purchase of Service (POS) standards
- Annual recertification
- Quarterly and annual reporting requirements
- Service documentation matching authorized services
- Billing compliance

### Incident Reporting
Required reporting timeframes:
- **Immediate**: Life-threatening situations, hospitalization, death
- **24 hours**: Serious injury, suspected abuse/neglect, law enforcement involvement
- **7 days**: Minor incidents requiring documentation

Report to:
- Regional Center (as vendored entity)
- Community Care Licensing (when applicable)
- Law enforcement (when criminal activity suspected)

### Common Consumer Population
Most consumers served have:
- Intellectual disabilities (formerly "developmental disabilities")
- Behavioral support needs
- Co-occurring mental health conditions
- May require specialized support plans

### Service Settings
- Consumers live in their own homes (owned, leased, or family home)
- NOT group homes or congregate care facilities
- Services delivered in community/natural settings
- Support is individualized and person-centered
