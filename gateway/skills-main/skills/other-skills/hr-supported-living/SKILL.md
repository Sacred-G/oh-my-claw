# HR & Compliance Skill for Supported Living Services

This skill provides comprehensive guidance for creating HR policies, compliance documentation, operational procedures, and business documents specific to California Title 17 supported living agencies that serve consumers through Regional Center vendor agreements.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when the user requests:
- Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
- HR policies for new hires or staff management
- Statements of Work (SOWs) for consulting services
- Compliance forms and documentation
- Operational procedures and workflows
- Title 17 compliance materials
- Regional Center vendor documentation
- Training materials for Direct Support Staff

## Core Principles

### Correct Terminology
Always use proper terminology as defined in `references/terminology.md`:
- **Consumers** (never clients, patients, or residents)
- **Direct Support Staff (DSS)** (never Direct Support Professional/DSP)
- **Title 17** regulations (NOT Title 22)
- **Intellectual disability** (current preferred term)
- **Eight Rights of Medication Observation** (not administration)

### Regulatory Framework
All documents must align with:
- California Title 17 Community Care Licensing regulations
- Regional Center Purchase of Service (POS) standards
- Person-centered planning principles
- Consumer rights and dignity focus

### Document Quality Standards
- Use clear, professional language
- Include all required compliance elements
- Provide specific procedures (not vague guidelines)
- Ensure consistency with Title 17 requirements
- Use person-first, respectful language

## Workflow

### Step 1: Identify Document Type
Determine what the user needs:
- **SOP**: Step-by-step operational procedure
- **Policy**: HR or organizational policy statement
- **SOW**: Consulting services agreement
- **Compliance Form**: Required documentation template
- **Operational Procedure**: Workflow or process guide

### Step 2: Load Relevant References
Based on document type, review the appropriate reference files:

**For all documents:**
- `references/terminology.md` - Ensure correct terminology throughout

**For SOPs and operational procedures:**
- `references/document_templates.md` - Standard structures and formats
- `references/operational_procedures.md` - Common workflows and best practices
- `references/title17_compliance.md` - Regulatory requirements

**For HR policies:**
- `references/document_templates.md` - Policy template structure
- `references/operational_procedures.md` - Performance management and training requirements
- `references/title17_compliance.md` - Staffing and training requirements

**For compliance forms:**
- `references/document_templates.md` - Form structure guidelines
- `references/title17_compliance.md` - Documentation requirements
- `references/operational_procedures.md` - Related procedures

**For SOWs:**
- `references/document_templates.md` - SOW template structure
- `references/title17_compliance.md` - Compliance considerations for consulting work

### Step 3: Create Document
Follow the appropriate template structure from `references/document_templates.md` and incorporate:
- Correct terminology from `references/terminology.md`
- Relevant compliance requirements from `references/title17_compliance.md`
- Best practices from `references/operational_procedures.md`

### Step 4: Compliance Check
Before finalizing, verify:
- ✅ Uses "consumers" not "clients"
- ✅ Uses "Direct Support Staff" not "Direct Support Professional"
- ✅ References Title 17 (not Title 22) where applicable
- ✅ Includes person-first language
- ✅ Addresses relevant Title 17 requirements
- ✅ Includes appropriate review/approval sections
- ✅ Has clear documentation and retention requirements

### Step 5: Format and Present
Create the document in the appropriate format:
- **Word documents (.docx)**: For policies, SOPs that will be edited
- **PDF**: For final versions requiring signatures
- **Markdown**: For collaborative editing or web publishing

## Common Document Requests

### New Hire Policy
Include: background clearance requirements, orientation timeline (within 7 days per Title 17), required training, probationary period, documentation requirements.

### Standard Operating Procedure
Include: purpose, scope, responsibilities, step-by-step procedure, documentation requirements, related policies, approval signatures.

### Statement of Work
Include: scope, deliverables, timeline, client responsibilities, compliance requirements, fees, acceptance criteria, terms and conditions.

### Incident Report Form
Include: consumer information, incident details, witness statements, immediate actions taken, notifications made, follow-up required, signatures.

### Medication Observation Log
Include: Eight Rights verification checklist, consumer information, medication details, observation documentation, refusal documentation, staff signature.

## Quality Standards

### Professional Tone
- Clear and direct
- Respectful and person-centered
- Free of jargon (or jargon is defined)
- Appropriate for regulatory review

### Completeness
- All required sections included
- Specific procedures (not vague)
- Clear responsibilities assigned
- Documentation requirements stated

### Compliance
- Aligns with Title 17 regulations
- Meets Regional Center POS standards
- Includes consumer rights considerations
- Addresses mandated reporting when relevant
