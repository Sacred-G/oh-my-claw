# Document Templates and Structures

## Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) Template

### Structure
```
Title: [Descriptive Title]
Document ID: SOP-[Category]-[Number]
Effective Date: [Date]
Last Revised: [Date]
Approved By: [Name/Title]
Review Cycle: [Annual/Biannual/As Needed]

1. PURPOSE
   Brief statement of why this procedure exists

2. SCOPE
   Who/what this applies to (e.g., "All Direct Support Staff working with Centered Support Service consumers")

3. DEFINITIONS
   Key terms specific to this procedure

4. RESPONSIBILITIES
   Who is responsible for what aspects of this procedure

5. PROCEDURE
   Step-by-step instructions
   5.1 [First major step]
       5.1.1 [Sub-step]
       5.1.2 [Sub-step]
   5.2 [Second major step]
   
6. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
   What must be recorded and where

7. RELATED POLICIES/FORMS
   References to connected documents

8. REVISION HISTORY
   Date | Version | Changes | Approved By
```

### Common SOP Categories
- **Clinical/Service Delivery**: Medication observation, behavior support, crisis intervention
- **Administrative**: Time tracking, payroll, scheduling, documentation
- **Compliance**: Incident reporting, training requirements, background checks
- **Safety**: Emergency procedures, infection control, universal precautions
- **HR**: Onboarding, performance reviews, disciplinary actions

## Policy Document Template

### Structure
```
Policy Title: [Descriptive Title]
Policy Number: POL-[Department]-[Number]
Category: [HR/Operations/Clinical/Safety/Compliance]
Effective Date: [Date]
Review Date: [Date]

POLICY STATEMENT
Clear, concise statement of the policy (2-3 sentences)

RATIONALE/PURPOSE
Why this policy exists (regulatory compliance, best practice, risk management)

SCOPE
Who and what situations this policy covers

DEFINITIONS
Key terms used in this policy

POLICY DETAILS
Detailed explanation of policy requirements and expectations

PROCEDURES
Related procedures or reference to relevant SOPs

COMPLIANCE
Consequences of non-compliance, monitoring procedures

REFERENCES
- Title 17 regulations (if applicable)
- Regional Center POS standards (if applicable)
- Other relevant policies or legal requirements

APPROVAL
Approved by: [Name, Title]
Date: [Date]

REVISION HISTORY
Date | Version | Summary of Changes | Approved By
```

- Training and professional development
- Consumer rights and dignity
- Confidentiality and HIPAA compliance
- Medication observation protocols
- Incident reporting and investigation
- Grievance procedures

## Statement of Work (SOW) Template

### Structure
```
STATEMENT OF WORK

Client: [Company Name]
Project: [Brief Description]
Prepared By: [Your Name, Title]
Date: [Date]
Valid Through: [Date]

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
   Brief overview of services to be provided

2. SCOPE OF WORK
   2.1 Services Included
       - Detailed list of deliverables
       - Specific tasks and responsibilities
   
   2.2 Services Excluded
       - What is NOT included to avoid scope creep

3. DELIVERABLES
   Item | Description | Due Date | Format
   [List each deliverable]

4. TIMELINE
   Phase | Tasks | Duration | Completion Date
   [Project phases and milestones]

5. CLIENT RESPONSIBILITIES
   What the client must provide (access, documents, personnel availability, etc.)

6. ASSUMPTIONS
   Conditions assumed to be true for this SOW

7. COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS
   - Title 17 compliance considerations
   - Regional Center vendor standards
   - Industry best practices

8. FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS
   Service | Rate/Fee | Billing Schedule
   
9. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
   How deliverables will be evaluated and approved

10. TERMS AND CONDITIONS
    - Confidentiality
    - Intellectual property
    - Change management process
    - Termination clause

APPROVAL
Client: _____________________ Date: _______
Consultant: _________________ Date: _______
```

### Common SOW Services for Supported Living Consulting

- Title 17 compliance audit and remediation
- HR system implementation
- Staff training program development
- Incident reporting system setup
- Documentation review and improvement
- Regional Center vendor compliance preparation
- IT infrastructure assessment and recommendations

## Compliance Forms Common Structure

### Essential Elements
1. **Header**: Form title, form number, date, consumer name (if applicable)
2. **Purpose Statement**: Brief explanation of why form exists
3. **Data Fields**: Clear, specific fields with adequate space
4. **Instructions**: How to complete the form (can be on back or separate sheet)
5. **Signatures**: Who must sign and date
6. **Distribution**: Who receives copies
7. **Retention**: How long to keep on file

### Common Compliance Forms
- New Hire Checklist (background clearance, orientation completion, training records)
- Consumer Rights Acknowledgment
- Incident Report Form
- Medication Observation Log
- Training Attendance Record
- Staff Competency Assessment
- Grievance Form
- Emergency Contact Information
